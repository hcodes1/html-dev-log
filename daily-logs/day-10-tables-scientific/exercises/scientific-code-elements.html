Scientific Notation with Superscript and Subscript

The famous mathematical equation for Einstein's mass-energy equivalence is E=mc2. Here, '2' is rendered as a superscript, indicating an exponent.

Chemical formulas frequently use subscript to denote the number of atoms. For example, the chemical formula for water is H2O, and for sulfuric acid, it's H2SO4.

Footnotes often use superscript numbers, like in this sentence1, to refer to a citation or additional information.
